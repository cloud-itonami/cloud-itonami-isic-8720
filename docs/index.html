Residential Care Governor

ISIC Rev.5 8720 • Open Business Blueprint

Overview

An open-source business blueprint for behavioral-health residential-care operators. This repository implements a Behavioral Care Governor—a sealed LLM advisor paired with independent safety gates—to guide resident intake, jurisdiction assessment, medication-adherence screening, treatment-plan finalization, and crisis-response finalization.

Built on langgraph-clj StateGraph (portable .cljc, supervised superstep loop, interrupts, Datomic/in-memory checkpoints), the actor seals a BehavioralOps-LLM into a single node and wraps it with spec-basis validation, evidence-completeness checks, supervision-ratio sufficiency, medication-adherence-flag evaluation, and an immutable audit ledger. Hard gates ensure no treatment plan or crisis response is ever finalized without human sign-off from a licensed behavioral-health professional.

Any qualified, licensed behavioral-health facility operator can fork, deploy, run, improve, and sell this service in their jurisdiction.

What It Does

  • Resident intake: Structured intake capture with jurisdiction-specific checklisting
  • Jurisdiction assessment: HARD-gated on official spec-basis citations from behavioral.facts/catalog
  • Medication-adherence screening: Unconditional evaluation that can HARD-hold a proposal
  • Treatment-plan finalization: Draft proposals with evidence-complete gates; human approval required
  • Crisis-response finalization: Supervision-ratio validation, evidence gates, double-finalization guards
  • Audit ledger: Immutable record of every intake, assessment, screening, and finalization decision

Safety by Construction

No autonomous finalization. Both layers enforce human approval: the Behavioral Care Governor's :actuation/finalize-treatment-plan and :actuation/finalize-crisis-response high-stakes gates, plus the phase table which never puts finalization operations into any phase's :auto set.

Hard rejection on: fabricated jurisdiction citations · incomplete evidence · insufficient supervision ratio (maximum direction) · unresolved medication-adherence flags · double-finalization attempts.
